# agentme-core Scope Overview

## Overview

The `agentme-core` scope holds all meta-governance for the `agentme` scope: writing standards, content conventions, authoring guidance, and scope identity. It governs how policies and skills in `agentme` are written and maintained.

**This scope is not distributed to consumers.** The `agentme` preset ships only the content of the `agentme` scope; `agentme-core` stays internal to the agentme repository.

## Content

### What this scope covers

- The identity, purpose, and target audience of the `agentme` scope.
- Writing standards specific to `agentme` content: required structure, tone, example requirements, and coverage of design, implementation, and testing.
- Guidance on what belongs in `agentme` and what does not.

### What this scope does NOT cover

- Actual rules, constraints, or decisions for developers to follow in their projects — those belong in `agentme`.
- Framework-level rules — those are in `_core`.
